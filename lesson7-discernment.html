A Closer Look at Discernment

Your Quality Control System for AI Collaboration

If Description is about clearly communicating what you want, Discernment is about deciding whether what you get back actually meets your needs.

✅

Product Discernment

Judging the quality of the final output.

This is the most direct form of evaluation. It requires your domain expertise to judge the AI-generated content against your standards.

Evaluation Checklist:

  • Is it factually accurate and free of hallucinations?
  • Is it appropriate for the intended audience and purpose?
  • Is it coherent, logical, and well-structured?
  • Does it fully meet the requirements you specified?
AI Output: "Our new app is good."

Your Feedback: "This is too generic."
AI Output: "Our new app is good."

Your Feedback: "This slogan is factually correct but lacks impact (Product Discernment). Let's revise. Generate five slogans that highlight the time-saving feature for busy professionals aged 30-45 (Better Description)."
⚙️

Process Discernment

Judging the quality of the AI's reasoning.

For complex tasks, you must assess not just the final answer, but *how* the AI got there. This ensures you can trust its work, especially when the correct answer isn't obvious.

Watch For:

  • Logical errors or flawed reasoning steps.
  • Lapses in attention (e.g., forgetting an earlier instruction).
  • Getting "stuck" on a rejected idea or detail.
  • Circular reasoning that doesn't advance the solution.
"No, not that idea again."
"I'm noticing you're re-inserting elements of the 'eco-friendly' angle we decided to discard three messages ago (Process Discernment). Please ignore all previous brainstorming and focus only on developing the 'luxury a-la-carte' option from here on out."
🎭

Performance Discernment

Judging the quality of the interaction itself.

This is about the *style* of collaboration. Is the AI behaving like the partner you need for this specific task? The process is *what* it's doing; performance is *how* it's interacting with you while doing it.

Ask Yourself:

  • Is its communication style helpful and efficient?
  • Is it asking too many questions when you need speed?
  • Is it being too brief when you need comprehensive detail?
  • Does it respond well to feedback and direction?
"Just give me the answers."
"Your detailed clarifying questions are helpful for formal reports, but for this task, your performance is too slow (Performance Discernment). Please switch roles. Act as a rapid-fire brainstormer. Do not ask for clarification; generate ten raw, unfiltered ideas based on my last prompt."

The Discernment-Description Feedback Loop

🔍 Discernment Flags a Problem → 💬 Better Description Provides a Solution → 🎯 Leading to a Higher Quality Output

💡 Beyond Feedback: Rethinking Delegation

When you consistently find flaws in the AI's output or process, the problem might not be your description. Your discernment should trigger a higher-level question:

"Am I using the right tool for this job, or approaching this problem in the right way?"

Sometimes, the solution isn't a better prompt, but a different tool or a completely different strategy.
