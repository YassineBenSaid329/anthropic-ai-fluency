A Closer Look at Description

The Art and Science of Communicating with AI

Core Idea: "AI tools are not databases or vending machines. They are interactive systems that can behave differently in different contexts... You need to explain how you want the AI to behave to get the best results."

📦

Product Description

Clearly defining WHAT you want the AI to create.

Instead of making the AI guess your intentions, provide a detailed "recipe" for the final output. The quality of the result is directly proportional to the clarity of your request.

Key Questions to Answer:

  • What is the context for this work?
  • What is the specific task you want the AI to perform?
  • What format should the output be (e.g., email, table, JSON)?
  • Who is the intended audience?
  • What style or tone is appropriate?
Write an email to my team about the new vacation policy.
CONTEXT: Our company is updating its vacation policy for 2024.
TASK: Draft an internal email to all employees.
AUDIENCE: All company employees (diverse roles).
STYLE: Professional, clear, and positive.
FORMAT: A well-structured email with a clear subject line, bullet points for key changes, and a concluding paragraph encouraging questions.
⚙️

Process Description

Guiding HOW the AI assistant approaches your request.

Specifying the "how" is often more important than the "what." You can provide step-by-step instructions (a cookbook), general guidance (a manual), or demonstrations (examples) to steer the AI's reasoning.

Key Questions to Answer:

  • Is there specific data or source material it must use?
  • Are there specific issues or points it must address in order?
  • Is there a specific workflow or technique to follow (e.g., SWOT analysis)?
  • Should it show its work or provide step-by-step reasoning?
Summarize this article for me.

[Article text pasted here]
Analyze the attached article by following this exact process:
1. Identify & Extract: Pull out the single main thesis statement.
2. List Key Arguments: Create a numbered list of the top 3 supporting arguments.
3. Find a Counterargument: Identify one potential weakness or counterargument mentioned by the author.
4. Final Summary: Synthesize the above points into a single, concise paragraph (under 100 words).
🎭

Performance Description

Defining the BEHAVIORAL style of the interaction.

Define the role you want the AI to play. Is it a neutral summarizer, a creative brainstormer, a critical debater? Setting its "persona" transforms it from a generic tool into a specialized thinking partner.

Key Questions to Answer:

  • Should it challenge your assumptions or follow your lead?
  • Should it be expansive and detailed or concise and to-the-point?
  • Are you narrowing down to one answer or exploring possibilities?
  • Should it explain its reasoning ("chain of thought") or just give the answer?
What do you think of my idea to launch a new podcast?
ROLE: Act as an experienced, critical venture capitalist.
BEHAVIOR: Your goal is to find the flaws in my idea. Be brutally honest. Do not offer encouragement. Challenge every assumption I've made.
TASK: Read my podcast idea below, and provide a list of the top 5 reasons it will likely fail.

[My podcast idea here]

The Goal: A Finely-Tuned Partner

By mastering Product, Process, and Performance description, you move beyond simple prompting and start conducting a symphony. You transform a generic assistant into a thinking partner that is precisely calibrated to your specific needs and context.
