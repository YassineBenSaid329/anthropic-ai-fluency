A Closer Look at Delegation

Lesson 4: Strategic Distribution of Labor

Effective AI collaboration isn't about handing over the wheel and calling it a day. It's about making thoughtful choices and delegating work that leverages the unique strengths of both human and artificial intelligence.

The Expert First, the Delegator Second

The cornerstone of good delegation isn't about AI—it's about your own expertise. Before involving AI, you must clearly define your vision. Without a clear goal, even the most advanced AI won't get you where you need to go.

The Three Elements of Delegation

1. Problem Awareness

Clearly defining your goals and understanding exactly what work is needed before bringing AI into the picture.

2. Platform Awareness

Working knowledge of available AI systems. Understanding which models prioritize speed, depth, accuracy, or creativity.

3. Task Delegation

The strategic process of dividing work between Automation, Augmentation, Agency, or Human-only execution.

Strategic Questions for Delegation

  • Vision: What does success look like for this specific project?
  • Typology: Is this task simple/time-consuming (Automation) or complex/uncertain (Augmentation)?
  • Boundary: Are there areas of critical judgment that should remain exclusively human?
  • Landscape: Have I experimented with different systems to find the one best suited for this task?

"The most effective AI collaborators are experts in their fields first and AI delegators second."
