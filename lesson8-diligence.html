A Closer Look at Diligence

Ensuring Ethical, Safe, and Responsible AI Use

🚗 The Driving Analogy

Using AI is like driving a car. You don't just focus on getting from A to B (effectiveness). You also follow traffic rules, consider safety, and remain aware of your impact on others. Diligence is the responsible driving of AI.

🏗️

Creation Diligence

Choosing your tools and methods thoughtfully.

This is about being critical and intentional *before* you even start a major project. It requires you to consider the broader context of the AI systems you work with.

Key Questions to Ask:

  • How was this system trained and what data was used?
  • Who owns the data I am inputting right now?
  • How is my privacy and the privacy of others being protected?
  • Does using this tool align with my personal or organizational values?
In Practice: Before pasting sensitive company data into an AI chat, a diligent user first checks their organization's policies on third-party AI use and verifies the service's data protection and privacy terms.
📢

Transparency Diligence

Being open and honest about AI's role.

This is about maintaining trust by being forthright about how AI was involved in your work. People have a right to know when AI plays a significant role in content or decisions that affect them.

Key Questions to Ask:

  • Who needs to know about AI's role in this work?
  • How and when should I communicate this involvement?
  • What level of detail makes sense to share?
In Practice: After using an AI to help draft a team proposal, a diligent team member adds a footnote or comment saying, "Initial draft and structure generated with AI assistance, then manually edited and verified by [Your Name]."
✅

Deployment Diligence

Taking ownership of the final output.

When you use, share, or publish AI-assisted content, you are the one accountable for its quality and impact. The AI is a tool; you are the final checkpoint and publisher.

Your Responsibility Checklist:

  • Have I verified all facts, figures, and sources?
  • Have I checked for and corrected any potential biases?
  • Do I have the necessary usage rights for this content?
  • Can I confidently stand behind this work as my own?
In Practice: A journalist uses an AI to summarize interview transcripts for an article. Before publishing, they meticulously cross-reference every summary point with the original audio to ensure complete accuracy, just as they would if a human assistant had done the work.

⚠️ The Unspoken Rule: You Are Accountable

When you share AI-generated content with the world, you, not the AI, are ultimately responsible for its accuracy, appropriateness, and impact.
